If you already bill and provision hosting through WHMCS, you do not need a separate
system to start selling domains. The **CentralNic Reseller module for WHMCS** plugs
domain registration, transfers, renewals, DNS and SSL straight into the platform
your customers and staff already use — under your own brand, from the admin and
client areas you know. This guide takes you along the happy path: install the
module, connect your account, set pricing, prove it with a test order, and turn on
automated renewals.

It is written for hosting companies and resellers who want to be live quickly. The
module is the shortest route from "we run WHMCS" to "we sell domains", and with a
funded account already in place you can be taking orders in **under an hour**.

:::tip[By the end of this guide you will have]
- The **CentralNic Reseller module installed** in your WHMCS instance
- Your **CentralNic Reseller account connected** with your credentials
- **Pricing and TLD availability configured** so customers can find and buy domains
- A **test registration completed** end to end
- **Automated renewals switched on** so domains stay live without manual work
:::

:::note[First things first]
The module connects a WHMCS installation to a **funded, secured CentralNic
Reseller account** — it is not the account setup itself. If you have not funded
your account, created users and enabled security yet, work through
[Your First Day as a CentralNic Reseller](../../start-here/your-first-day/) first.
It takes about 30 minutes and applies to every integration method.

Not sure WHMCS is the right path for you? See
[Which Integration Method Should I Choose?](../../start-here/which-integration-method/).
:::

## Before you start

You need two things in place:

- A **running WHMCS installation** you can log into as an administrator and upload
  files to.
- A **funded CentralNic Reseller account** with credentials — the account name and
  a user login (a user logs in as `accountname:username`, as covered in
  [Your First Day](../../start-here/your-first-day/)).

The CentralNic Reseller registrar module has the WHMCS module ID **`cnic`**. It is
distributed as the **WHMCS CNIC Software Bundle**, an all-in-one package that adds
domains, DNS, DNSSEC and SSL management to WHMCS, along with helper addons for
domain search, importing and monitoring.

## 1. Install the CentralNic Reseller module

The module is published on the
[WHMCS Marketplace](https://marketplace.whmcs.com/product/6677-centralnic-reseller-software-bundle-for-whmcs)
and on
[GitHub](https://github.com/centralnicgroup-opensource/rtldev-middleware-whmcs).
Installation is a file upload followed by activation inside WHMCS.

<Steps>

1. **Download the bundle.** Get the latest
   [WHMCS CNIC Software Bundle](https://github.com/centralnicgroup-opensource/rtldev-middleware-whmcs/raw/main/whmcs-cnic-bundle.zip)
   (`whmcs-cnic-bundle.zip`).

2. **Extract and upload.** Unzip the archive and upload its contents into your
   WHMCS `modules` directory. The registrar module is placed under
   `modules/registrars/cnic`.

3. **Activate the registrar.** In the WHMCS admin, open your domain registrar
   settings, find **CentralNic Reseller** in the list of available registrars, and
   activate it.

</Steps>

:::tip[Follow the official installation guide alongside these steps]
For the exact WHMCS menu paths, version requirements, upgrade instructions and the
setup of the bundled addons, keep the module's own
[Installation Guide](https://support.centralnicreseller.com/hc/en-gb/sections/13438422570781-WHMCS)
open as you go. It is the authoritative, always-current reference for the module.
:::

:::note[Running more than one account?]
If you need to run several CentralNic Reseller accounts or brand variants side by
side in one WHMCS installation, the bundle includes **wrapper modules** (`cnic1`,
`cnic2`, and so on). Extract each wrapper so its path is `modules/registrars/cnicX`
— WHMCS will not detect it anywhere else.
:::

## 2. Connect your CentralNic Reseller account

With the registrar activated, tell it which account to use. In the module's
configuration screen, enter your **CentralNic Reseller account credentials** and
save. WHMCS will then route domain checks, registrations, transfers and renewals
through your account.

:::caution[Test against OT&E before you sell to real customers]
CentralNic Reseller gives you a free **OT&E (Operational Test & Evaluation)**
sandbox where nothing is billed and no live domains are touched. Point the module
at your OT&E account credentials while you set everything up and place test orders,
then switch to your production credentials when you are ready to go live. See
[Your First Day](../../start-here/your-first-day/) for how OT&E works and
[Moving from OT&E to Production](../../start-here/moving-from-ote-to-production/)
for the switch-over. Confirm the exact credential fields in the
[Installation Guide](https://support.centralnicreseller.com/hc/en-gb/sections/13438422570781-WHMCS).
:::

## 3. Configure pricing and TLD availability

Customers can only buy the TLDs you offer, at the prices you set. WHMCS manages
this in its own **domain pricing** area, where you choose which extensions to sell
and set your registration, transfer and renewal prices — with CentralNic Reseller
selected as the registrar for each TLD.

<Steps>

1. **Decide which TLDs to offer.** Add the extensions you want to sell to your
   WHMCS domain pricing list.

2. **Assign CentralNic Reseller as the registrar** for each of those TLDs so orders
   route to your account.

3. **Set your retail prices.** Your wholesale cost comes from your CentralNic
   Reseller pricescale (see **Account Balance → Services & Prices** in the
   [Control Panel](https://account.centralnicreseller.com/)); your margin is the
   difference between that and what you charge in WHMCS.

</Steps>

:::tip[Fewer failed orders on demanding TLDs]
The module is TLD-aware: it handles the extra registry fields and validation that
TLDs such as `.de`, `.us` and `.dk` require, and supports internationalised domain
names (IDNs). That means more registrations and transfers go through the first
time. The bundled **CNIC Admin Config** addon lets you set default values for these
additional domain fields.
:::

## 4. Run a test registration

Before you announce domains to your customers, prove the whole chain end to end —
search, order, payment and provisioning — with one test registration.

<Steps>

1. **Search for a domain** in WHMCS, either through the admin area or your client
   area storefront, and confirm availability results come back.

2. **Place the order** for an available, low-cost domain and take it through your
   normal WHMCS checkout and payment flow.

3. **Confirm it provisioned.** Check that WHMCS reports the domain as active and
   that it appears in your CentralNic Reseller account.

</Steps>

:::tip[Do this in OT&E first]
Running the test order against your OT&E credentials means you can exercise the
full flow without spending real money. When the OT&E result looks right, the same
order against production should behave identically — which is exactly the
confidence you want before going live.
:::

## 5. Enable automated renewals

The point of running domains inside WHMCS is that they largely look after
themselves. WHMCS handles renewal orders, invoicing and provisioning on its
automation (cron) schedule, and the CentralNic Reseller account applies its own
renewal behaviour on top.

- In **WHMCS**, make sure domain **auto-renewal** and the automation cron are
  enabled so renewal invoices are raised and paid renewals are provisioned on time.
- In **CentralNic Reseller**, confirm your renewal mode. **AUTORENEW** renews
  domains automatically before they expire — the safe default for most resellers —
  and is set under **Account → Settings → General** in the Control Panel (see
  [Your First Day](../../start-here/your-first-day/)).

:::caution[Keep your prepay balance topped up]
Renewals draw on your CentralNic Reseller balance in real time. A balance that runs
dry can cause domains to lapse even when WHMCS has collected payment from your
customer. Set a **low-balance warning** in the Control Panel so you are notified in
good time.
:::
